Meiji Shrine (Meiji Jingu)

  • Meiji Shrine (Meiji Jingu): Meiji Shrine is a serene and historically significant Shinto shrine located in the heart of Tokyo. It was established in 1920 and is dedicated to Emperor Meiji and Empress Shoken, known for their role in Japan's modernization.
  • Architecture: The shrine's entrance features a massive torii gate and a long approach lined with towering trees, creating a peaceful atmosphere. The main shrine buildings are made of Japanese cypress and copper, following traditional architectural styles.
  • Surrounding Forest: Meiji Shrine is surrounded by a lush forest, providing a tranquil retreat from the bustling city. Visitors can take leisurely walks through the forested areas and explore the Inner Garden.
  • Visiting Tips: Visitors can participate in traditional rituals, write wishes on wooden ema plaques, and witness weddings and ceremonies. Meiji Shrine is also known for its seasonal festivals and events.
  • Harajuku: The shrine's location in Harajuku makes it easily accessible for visitors who want to explore the trendy Harajuku district with its unique fashion and vibrant street culture.
  • Location: Meiji Shrine is situated in Shibuya, Tokyo, near Yoyogi Park, and can be reached via public transportation.
